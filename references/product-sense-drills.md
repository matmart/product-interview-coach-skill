# Product Sense Sprint Drills — Frozen Context Library

## How Sprint Drills Work

Each drill isolates **one sub-skill** with frozen context. The candidate doesn't need to do the full case — they practice one move in 3-5 minutes. The coach scores only that section.

**Protocol**:
1. Coach provides the frozen context + prompt
2. Candidate answers in 3-5 minutes (spoken, not written)
3. Coach scores on relevant rubric dimensions only
4. Coach provides: Insufficient Phrases → Why This Failed → Better Approach → Top 2 Focus Areas

## Drill Type 1: Segmentation

**Task**: Given a product and goal, provide 3 segments using a named heuristic, prioritize one, and defend why not the others.

### Drill 1A — Instagram Engagement
**Frozen context**: Instagram is seeing flat growth in content creation (posts, Reels, Stories) among US users. The team wants to increase content creation.
**Prompt**: Segment Instagram's user base for this problem. Name your heuristic, give 3 segments, prioritize one, and defend why not the others.

### Drill 1B — WhatsApp Business
**Frozen context**: WhatsApp Business wants to increase the number of small businesses that actively use the platform to communicate with customers.
**Prompt**: Segment small businesses on WhatsApp. Name your heuristic, give 3 segments, prioritize one, defend why not.

### Drill 1C — Uber Eats Retention
**Frozen context**: Uber Eats has strong first-order rates but 60-day retention is declining. The team wants to improve repeat ordering.
**Prompt**: Segment Uber Eats customers for this retention problem. Name your heuristic, give 3 segments, prioritize one, defend why not.

### Drill 1D — YouTube Shorts
**Frozen context**: YouTube Shorts is growing in views but creator monetization satisfaction is low. The team wants to improve creator retention on Shorts.
**Prompt**: Segment Shorts creators. Name your heuristic, give 3 segments, prioritize one, defend why not.

### Drill 1E — Facebook Groups
**Frozen context**: Facebook Groups engagement is declining. The team wants to revitalize community participation.
**Prompt**: Segment Groups users. Name your heuristic, give 3 segments, prioritize one, defend why not.

## Drill Type 2: Bottleneck Identification

**Task**: Given a product, user segment, and 3 pain points, name the single bottleneck and defend why it's the one that most limits success.

### Drill 2A — Airbnb First-Time Guest
**Frozen context**: Focus on first-time Airbnb guests in the US booking a weekend trip.
**Pain points provided**:
1. Overwhelming number of listings — hard to decide
2. Anxiety about host reliability — "will it look like the photos?"
3. Complex pricing — cleaning fees, service fees make the total unclear until checkout

**Prompt**: Which is THE bottleneck? Why? What happens if you solve it?

### Drill 2B — LinkedIn Job Seekers
**Frozen context**: Focus on mid-career professionals (5-10 years experience) actively looking for a new role on LinkedIn.
**Pain points provided**:
1. Job recommendations feel generic — not aligned with actual career goals
2. Application process is a black hole — no feedback after applying
3. Profile doesn't differentiate them from thousands of similar candidates

**Prompt**: Which is THE bottleneck? Why? What happens if you solve it?

### Drill 2C — Spotify Podcast Listeners
**Frozen context**: Focus on casual podcast listeners (listen 1-2x per week) who Spotify wants to convert to daily listeners.
**Pain points provided**:
1. Discovery is broken — hard to find new podcasts they'll like
2. Episodes are too long for available time
3. No social component — can't see what friends are listening to

**Prompt**: Which is THE bottleneck? Why? What happens if you solve it?

### Drill 2D — Google Maps Local Business
**Frozen context**: Focus on local business owners who have claimed their Google Maps listing but rarely update it.
**Pain points provided**:
1. Updating the listing is tedious — too many fields, unclear what matters
2. No clear ROI — business owners don't know if their listing drives customers
3. Negative reviews are stressful and feel uncontrollable

**Prompt**: Which is THE bottleneck? Why? What happens if you solve it?

### Drill 2E — Marketplace Seller Activation
**Frozen context**: Focus on casual sellers on Facebook Marketplace who listed once but never listed again.
**Pain points provided**:
1. Listing creation is manual and time-consuming
2. Messages from buyers are flaky — they ghost after initial interest
3. Pricing is guesswork — no guidance on what's a fair price

**Prompt**: Which is THE bottleneck? Why? What happens if you solve it?

## Drill Type 6: "Why Not" Defense

**Task**: The coach states a candidate's chosen direction, then aggressively argues for an alternative. Candidate must defend their choice under pressure.

### Drill 6A
**Setup**: "You chose to focus on skeptical buyers on Marketplace."
**Challenge**: "Aren't power users more valuable? They drive 80% of transactions. Why waste time on people who barely buy?"

### Drill 6B
**Setup**: "You chose an AI verification system as your solution."
**Challenge**: "That's expensive and complex. A simple seller rating system would ship in 2 weeks and cover 90% of the problem. Why are you over-engineering this?"

### Drill 6C
**Setup**: "You chose lifecycle stage as your segmentation heuristic."
**Challenge**: "But lifecycle stage doesn't tell you WHY they churned. Two churned users could have completely different problems. Isn't constraint-based segmentation more useful here?"

### Drill 6D
**Setup**: "You chose to focus on the discovery phase of the journey."
**Challenge**: "The data shows most drop-off happens at transaction, not discovery. Users find stuff — they just don't buy. Why are you solving a problem that isn't the bottleneck?"

### Drill 6E
**Setup**: "You said the biggest risk is false confidence from AI scores."
**Challenge**: "That's a solvable engineering problem, not a product risk. What's the REAL risk — the one that would kill this product even if the AI works perfectly?"

## Drill Scheduling Guidance

**For new product sense practitioners:**
- Week 1: Mission Statement + Needs vs. Problems + Paint the User drills (build foundations)
- Week 2-3: Segmentation + Bottleneck drills (build empathy and convergence)
- Week 3-4: Solution Prioritization + V1 Walkthrough drills (build craft)
- Week 5+: Full mock + Redo + Pressure + Why Not Defense (build endurance and fluency)

**For experienced practitioners sharpening specific skills:**
- Run the drill type that matches their weakest dimension on the rubric
- After 3 reps at a drill type, switch to a full mock to test integration
- Mission Statement and Needs vs. Problems drills are useful warmups even for experienced practitioners — they sharpen the foundation that every other section builds on
